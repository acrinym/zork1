## 1.1 Further earned alternate solutions — after mechanism priorities

Candidate actions must satisfy all of these:

- use existing visible objects or deliberately added coherent objects;
- require preparation, timing, positioning, or genuine vulnerability;
- preserve original solutions;
- have meaningful failure consequences;
- avoid duplicate treasure or parallel puzzle state;
- remain funny because the physics is ridiculous, not because the parser grants arbitrary wishes.

Potential investigations:

- tricking or restraining another actor only where motives and objects support it;
- catching, redirecting, cushioning, or deliberately breaking falling objects;
- using fire, water, rope, containers, or machinery in unusual but material-consistent ways;
- deceptive uses of voice, notes, gifts, and scenery that create temporary—not universal—openings.
